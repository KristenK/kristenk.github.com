How to properly cite sources

Citing books and journal articles is fairly straight forward, but it may be that you have to cite an uncommon source, or use a type of citation style specific to a particular journal and are not sure where to look. Bellow are a few uncommon examples and a list of further resources.

Dissertations and Theses

APA

Pecore, J. T. (2004). Sounding the spirit of Cambodia: The living tradition of Khmer music and dance-drama in a Washington, DC community (Doctoral dissertation). Retrieved from Dissertations and Theses database. (UMI No. 3114720)

Chicago

Choi, Mihwa. “Contesting Imaginaires in Death Rituals during the Northern Song Dynasty.” PhD diss., University of Chicago, 2008.

MLA

Bishop, Karen Lynn. Documenting Institutional Identity: Strategic Writing in the IUPUI Comprehensive Campaign. Diss. Purdue University, 2002. Ann Arbor: UMI, 2004. Print.

Film

APA

Spurlock, M. (Director). (2004). Supersize Me: A Film of Epic Proportions [Motion picture]. (Available from Samuel Goldwyn Films, 1133 Broadway, New York, NY 10010).

Chicago

Spurlock, Morgan. Supersize Me: A Film of Epic Proportions DVD. Directed by Morgan Spurlock. New York: Samuel Goldwyn Films, 2004.

MLA

Supersize Me: A Film of Epic Proportions Morgan Spurlock. Samuel Goldwyn Films, 2004. DVD.

Legal Materials

APA

Supreme Courtcase: Name v. Name. Vol. number U.S. Page number. (Year).

  • United States v. Lane, 474 U.S. 438 (1986).

U.S. Constitutions: Abbreviated name of Constitution pt. Part number (if any), amend. Amendment number in Roman numerals, § Section number.

  • U.S. Const., amend. XIV, § 1.

Examples taken from Westfield State University Ely Library APA Style - Citing Legal Materials

Chicago

Court Decisions: United States v. Christmas, 222 F.3d 141, 145 (4th Cir. 2000).

United States Supreme Court decisions: AT&T Corp. v. Iowa Utils. Bd., 525 U.S. 366 (1999).

For more examples see Chicago Manual of Style - Legal and Public Documents

MLA

Court Cases: Name of the Case. Case Citation. Court--Supreme Court of the US or other federal or state court. Year. Source of Information. Medium of Publication. Date Accessed if found on Web.

  • Harper & Row v. Nation Enterprises. 471 U.S. 539. Supreme Court of the United States. 1985. LexisNexis Academic. Web. 19 Mar. 2010.

Example taken from Lynn University MLA Formatting Style Guide
