How to properly cite sources

Depending on the style of citation you use there will be different ways to cite different types of sources. In most cases books, journal articles, websites, and other types of sources require different formatting. Below are examples of different types of citation, in-text and bibliographic, for common sources.

MLA

Book

In The Great American Thing, Wanda Corn details the phenomenon of “Americanisme” or the “fascination of comparing modern America with old Europe.” (Corn, 49)

Corn, Wanda M. The Great American Thing: Modern Art and National Identity, 1915-1935. Berkeley: University of California, 1999. Print

Journal Article

Griffin, Farah. "Thirty Years of Black American Literature and Literary Studies: A Review." Journal of Black Studies. 35.2 (2004): 165-174. Print.

Website

Miranda, Carolina. "Seeing Red at MoMA." ARTnews. ARTnews LLC, 09 Feb. 2012. Web. 08 May 2013.
