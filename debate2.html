American Journals

In the early 20th century, print culture was extremly important for the circulation of artistic ideas and theory. American journals such The New Masses, and The Liberator where some of the first to begin calling for a reform in American art. In the 1920s, theorist Lewis Mumford and artist Hugo Gellert were just some of the vocal critics of the decadence of late 19th and early 20th century American art. Max Eastman, Don Brown and Michael Gold all wrote extensively on proletarian art in which “everyday subject matter and the untrained critic were given credence.”
