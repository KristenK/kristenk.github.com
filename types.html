Types of Plagiarism

It could happen that you commit plagiarism without being aware of it. Even if you commit an offense unintentionally, you are still held accountable. It is your responsibility to be aware of what is considered plagiarism or not. Plagiarism.org identifies 10 common types of plagiarism to form what they call the "plagiarism spectrum"

The Plagiarism Spectrum was developed as a way to define and distinguish the common ways in which plagiarism can take form. The Spectrum makes these forms memorable by tagging the types with “Digital 2.0” monikers, a gesture that both acknowledges the role that the internet plays in instances of content copying and makes the types more meaningful for a generation of writers who are “digital natives.”plagiarism.org, Types of Plagiarism

Ranked from the most serious offence down, the types of plagiarism are:

Clone

Copying word for word another authors work and passing it off as your own.

CRTL + C

Mixing large portions of cloned text with your own words.

Find – Replace

Changing specific words and phrases in a text.

Remix

Paraphrasing from a variety of sources and mixing them together to create your paper.

Recycle

Heavily borrows from another work without citation.

Hybrid

A mix of properly cited sources and copied information

Mash up

Mixes copied material from a variety of sources

404 Error

Cites non-existent sources or contains misinformation about source.

Aggregator

Sources are properly cited but the paper contains almost no original content

Re-Tweet

Provides correct citations but relies to heavily on the original text’s wording and structure
