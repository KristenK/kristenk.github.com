2. Which example is correctly paraphrased?

Original Content

There can be no doubt that MacMonnies and Cassatt knew and admired Puvis’s work. But after looking closely at his work and that of other male and female Puvian muralists at the fair, it is clear that, while men accepted Puvis’s definition of utopia, women did not.Wanda M. Corn, Gender, American Art , Vol. 23, No. 1 (Spring 2009), pp. 25-26 Published by: The University of Chicago Press on behalf of the Smithsonian American Art Museum Article DOI: 10.1086/599058

Example 1

It’s obvious that MacMonnies and Cassatt were familiar with and admired Puvis’s paintings. Though after looking closely at his work and the work of other mural painters influenced by Puvis, it is clear that male artists accepted Puvis’s version of utopia, while women did not.

Example 2

Wanda Corn notes that MacMonnies and Cassatt “knew and admired Puvis’s work” (Corn, 25). But Corn also mentions that after comparing the work of male and female followers of Puvis, that the murals painted by male artists more closely followed utopian vision, whereas the female artists did not.
